Optical Character Recognition

How does OCR work?

The following steps are involved in OCR:

  1. Image Acquisition: The source document or image is scanned or captured using a digital camera or scanner, converting it into a digital image.
  2. Preprocessing: The OCR software performs various operations to enhance the image quality and remove any noise or artifacts. This may include deskewing (correcting tilted images), despeckling (removing small dots or imperfections), and enhancing contrast and brightness.
  3. Text Recognition: The OCR engine analyzes the processed image to recognize and interpret the text within it. There are two main approaches used for text recognition ( Pattern Matching & Feature Extraction)
  4. Postprocessing: Once the text has been recognized, the OCR software performs additional processing to refine the extracted text. This may include spell-checking, error correction, and formatting adjustments.
  5. Output: The final output of the OCR process is the extracted text, which can be saved as a searchable and editable document format such as PDF, Word, or plain text. Some OCR systems also provide options to preserve the original layout and formatting of the source document.
